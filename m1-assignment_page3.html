Bob Iger

Robert Allen Iger ( born February 10, 1951) is an American businessman who is the chief executive officer (CEO) of The Walt Disney Company. He previously served as president of ABC Television from 1994 to 1995 and as president and chief operating officer (COO) of Capital Cities/ABC from 1995 until Disney's acquisition of the company in 1996. Iger was named president and COO of Disney in 2000, and later succeeded Michael Eisner as CEO in 2005, after a successful effort by Roy E. Disney to oust Eisner.

Career

Early career

Iger began his media career in 1974 as the host of Campus Probe, a local television show. He joined the American Broadcasting Company (ABC) in 1974, eventually becoming the president of ABC Entertainment in 1989.

Disney CEO (2005~2020)

During his first term as CEO of Disney, Iger expanded the company's portfolio of intellectual properties and its presence in international markets. He oversaw the acquisitions of Pixar (2006), Marvel Entertainment (2009), Lucasfilm (2012), and 21st Century Fox (2019).

Disney CEO (2022~present)

In November 2022, Iger returned as CEO of Disney after Bob Chapek was ousted due to a series of controversies. Iger has since focused on improving the company's streaming business and its relationship with its employees.

Awards and honors

  • Time 100 (2006, 2019)
  • Ambassador of the Order of Arts and Letters (2012)
  • Honorary Knight Commander of the Order of the British Empire (2019)
